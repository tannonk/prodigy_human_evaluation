Conditional Text Generation Human Evaluation: HELP

Overview:

The purpose of the annotation schema is to compare the outputs from different models against each other.

Comparisons are performed in a randomised pair-wise fashion. The results of the pair-wise scoring will be used to derive an overall ranking of all models being tested. For this we will use the TrueScore ranking algotirthm.

Task: Review Response Generation

Each annotation example consists of a review text (above) and two generated response texts (below, displayed sibe-by-side).

The review title, if available, is displayed in bold and prefixed to the rest of the review body.

For each pair of response texts, we ask a single assessment question, e.g. 'which response is...?'. The assessment question is displayed below the review text.

And the winner is...

The user can drag the slider displayed below the response texts to the left or right to indicate the degree to which one response is better given the assessment question.

Moving the slider to the left means that the response on the left is preferred. Conversely, moving the slider to the right indicates that the response on the right is preferred.

The amount the slider is moved indicates approximately how much better the corresponding response is than the other response.

All the way to the left indicates that the response on the left is entirely (e.g. 100%) better than the response on the right. On the other hand, moving the slider only slightly to the left (e.g. 15%) would indicate the the response on the left is only slightly better than the response on the right.

What to do if there's no clear winner

If both response texts are suitable/acceptable given the assessment question, indicate a positive draw by setting the slider in the middle (default value = 0) and click the ACCEPT button.

If both response texts are unsuitable given the assessment question (i.e. both responses are highly unrelated) indicate a negative draw by setting the slider in the middle (default value = 0) and click the REJECT button.

Example 1

REVIEW: very good --- Very good hotel, with good service; the hotel rooms are very good. But the finish isn't perfect and could do with more attention. The staff were very friendly, welcoming and professional. The bar was everything you'd expect from Wetherspoons

RESPONSE A: Thank you for taking the time to leave us a review. We are pleased to hear you enjoyed your stay with us. We hope to welcome you back again soon.

RESPONSE B: Thank you for taking the time to write a review about your recent stay at Wetherspoons. I was pleased to read that you found the staff to be friendly, welcoming and professional and that the bar was everything you would expect from a hotel. I am sorry that the finish of the hotel was not to your liking and I will pass on your comments to the relevant departments. I hope that we will have the opportunity to welcome you back to the hotel in the near future. Kind regards, Michael Grange General Manager

In this example, RESPONSE A is freindly and appropriate given the tone of the review, however, it is also generic and fails to address any of the points raised in the review.

In contrast, RESPONSE B responds to specific points mentioned in the review, namely, the positive remark about the staff and the critique regarding the decor of the hotel. Therefore, RESPONSE B is much more specific to the input review and should be indicated by setting the slider's cursor to the right (~100%) and hitting ACCEPT.

Example 2

REVIEW: Amazing Hotel!! --- What a great hotel! My family & I just spend 4 days during Spring Break and had an amazing time. The rooms are beautifully renovated with a modern but cozy look. The rooms were very spacious and very clean. Having a kitchen in our room is very convinient when you have small children. The entire staff was so friendly and so welcoming. Great breakfast was included with lots of options for everyone. The location is perfectly located within the village. There is so much to do within the village and everything is a short walk away. This is definitely an annual family Spring Break getaway.

RESPONSE A: Thank you for taking the time to post a review of the Crystal Lodge following your recent visit to Whistler. I am pleased to read that you and your family enjoyed your stay with us. We look forward to welcoming you back again in the future. Warm regards, Ian Lowe

RESPONSE B: Thank you for taking the time to share your recent experience at the Crystal Lodge following your family's holiday. We are very pleased to read that you enjoyed your stay with us and we look forward to your next visit. Sincerely, Ian Lowe

In this example, RESPONSE A and RESPONSE B are very similar and both are rather generic. They both pick up on the fact that the review is related to a family holiday but they fail to mention any of the main points raised in the review, e.g. 'Spring Break', quality of the rooms, breakfast and location, etc. Therefore, you could argue that the generated responses are not specific to the review and REJECT both, setting the slider's cursor in the middle.

Caveats

A major challenge of Conditional Text Generation is hallucination, where the model generates content that is unsupported by the source. We are aware that our model outputs sometimes contain instances of hallucination and this will be investigated as part of future work. For the time being, feel free to ignore any obvious hallucination issues.

Finalising an annotation

Upon accepting or rejecting an annotation example, the score provided will be saved and the next annotation task will be displayed.
